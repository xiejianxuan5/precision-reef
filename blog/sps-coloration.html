Advanced SPS Coloration: The Science of Dynamic Spectrum.

Jan 30, 2026 12 min read

For decades, the reef-keeping hobby has focused on PAR (Photosynthetically Active Radiation). But as we move into 2026, the data shows that static lighting is the primary cause of metabolic stagnation in Acropora and other high-end SPS corals.

The Limitations of Static Light

In the wild, corals are subjected to constantly shifting spectral qualities. Clouds, turbidity, and tidal depth changes create a dynamic "light dance" that prevents photo-inhibition. Traditional LED controllers offer "ramps," but they remain linear and predictable—exactly what nature is not.

"Corals don't just need light; they need the complexity of natural flux to trigger pigment production."

PUR over PAR

PONTIS prioritizes PUR (Photosynthetically Usable Radiation). By isolating the exact absorption peaks of Chlorophyll A and C2, we eliminate the yellow-green spectrum that only serves to heat the water and promote algae growth. The result is a deeper, more iridescent fluorescence that is impossible to achieve with standard fixtures.
